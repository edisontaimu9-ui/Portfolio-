About

Clinical Dietitian. Tech Enthusiast.

My clinical training covered the full scope of dietetic practice — nutrition screening and assessment, medical nutrition therapy across acute and chronic conditions, enteral and parenteral support, community nutrition, and food-service management. I'm comfortable with the Nutrition Care Process from the first assessment through to ADIME documentation, and I've worked within multidisciplinary teams in both hospital and community settings across Malawi.

On the technology side, I gravitate toward problems that are concrete and consequential. Oasis CNST reflects that — it's not a side project, it's a clinical tool I'd want to use myself at the bedside. Building it taught me how to architect offline-first systems, design for intermittent connectivity, localise food composition data for the Malawian context, and make AI-assisted documentation feel useful rather than gimmicky.

What ties both sides together is the same instinct: find what the system is missing, then do something about it. I want to practice as a clinical dietitian and continue building tools that make evidence-based nutrition care more reachable — in the wards, in the community, and in every setting where it matters.
